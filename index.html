Chronicle: Fei-Fei Li, a pioneer in artificial intelligence

October 12 2024
Dr. Fei Fei Li’s new book is The Worlds I See: Curiosity, Exploration, and Discovery at the Dawn of AI.

Fei-Fei Li, a figure in the realm of Artificial Intelligence (AI), born in Beijing, China, she developed a deep interest in AI early in life and pursued it passionately. Her career took off at Stanford University, where she earned her Ph.D. and later joined as a faculty member. It was here that she spearheaded revolutionary research in computer vision, particularly focusing on image recognition and understanding. Her seminal work in developing ImageNet, a vast database crucial for training AI systems, became a cornerstone in the development of deep learning algorithms. Beyond academia, Fei-Fei Li played a pivotal role in shaping AI policy and ethics. Recognizing the transformative potential of AI, she advocated for responsible AI development, emphasizing the importance of diversity and inclusion in technology. Her efforts led to the establishment of the Stanford Human-Centered AI Institute, where interdisciplinary collaboration thrives under her guidance. As her influence expanded globally, Li co-founded AI4ALL, a nonprofit organization dedicated to increasing diversity and inclusion in AI education. Her commitment to democratizing AI education has empowered countless young minds, especially women and underrepresented minorities, to pursue careers in technology. Fei-Fei Li's accolades are numerous, including being elected to the National Academy of Engineering and receiving prestigious awards such as the Blavatnik National Award for Young Scientists. Her TED talks and public appearances continue to inspire a new generation of AI enthusiasts worldwide. Today, Fei-Fei Li's legacy endures not only through her technical contributions but also through her advocacy for ethical AI practices and her dedication to ultivating a more inclusive AI community. Her journey exemplifies a relentless pursuit of knowledge and a steadfast commitment to harnessing technology for the betterment of humanity.

Crime and Punishment

October 13 2024
Crime and Punishment by Fyodor Dostoevsky eBook por Joseph Cowley

Crime and punishment is a complex and multifaceted issue that has been the subject of much debate throughout history. At its core, it involves the commission of a criminal act and the imposition of punishment by the state in response. Crime and punishment systems vary widely from country to country, but all have the goal of maintaining social order and protecting citizens from harm. One of the key issues in crime and punishment is determining the appropriate punishment for a given crime. Some argue for a system of punishment that is focused on rehabilitation, in which the offender is given the opportunity to learn new skills and receive treatment for underlying problems that may have contributed to their criminal behavior. Others argue for a system of punishment that is focused on retribution, in which the offender is punished in proportion to the severity of their crime as a way to deter others from committing similar crimes. Still, others argue that a system of punishment should be focused on incapacitation, where the offender is removed from society so they are unable to commit further crimes. One of the most important aspect of crime and punishment is the process of how the criminal is judged and punished. Fair Trial, Due process and access to legal representation are critical components of the justice system. It allows the accused to defend themselves, prevent arbitrary or discriminatory decisions and to ensure that the punishment fits the crime. Additionally, the use of evidence-based methods to ensure the right people are being held accountable for their crime, in order to improve the effectiveness of the punishment and reduce recidivism. Furthermore, in recent years, the issue of mass incarceration has come to the forefront as a major problem. Many argue that the current system of punishment is overly punitive and that the high rate of incarceration in the United States and other countries is not only costly but also disproportionately affects marginalized communities. This has led to calls for criminal justice reform, with a focus on reducing the number of people incarcerated, and providing alternatives to traditional forms of punishment, such as community supervision, treatment, and diversion programs.

Critical Thinking and Media Literacy

October 13 2024

When we talk about media and information literacy we must mention that it seems to be necessary in the formation of a citizenry capable of using information critically. The growing phenomena of misinformation, as well as the effects of the media and especially social media on certain groups, forces us to analyze how this type of education helps to create critical thinking. Within the information and communication society, the development of information competence among students is essential, because it allows them to analyze and manage the information they receive from different sources. This, together with the appearance of phenomena such as fake news and the development of information and communication technologies (ICT), means that we have chosen this topic of study. The research questions we have asked ourselves are the following: how information competence is currently developing, what effects does a low development of this competence have and how does critical thinking influence its development. To answer these questions, we have used the systematic review as a methodology, analyzing a total of 17 articles extracted from the Web Of Science, SCOPUS and Google Scholar databases. The conclusions reached establish that information competence is not being developed adequately, due to aspects such as the lack of teacher training or the lack of invested resources. This causes a change in methodology to be carried out. Furthermore, a low development of this causes a series of negative effects, such as misinformation. It concludes by establishing that critical thinking is one of the main elements that make up informational competence, being necessary to achieve a correct development of this competence and a good construction of knowledge among students.
